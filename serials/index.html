My dad used to tell me he would toss books across the room just to force himself to stop reading, and that obsession definitely carried over to me. When I was young, I would lose days in novels. I probably spent more hours reading lore than actually playing any of the games they belonged to.
But my biggest storytelling addiction of all was web serials. I've been quietly reading them for over 15 years, yet it's remained a very underground niche, and mentioning them tends to just elicit polite smiles and lukewarm curiosity. They're definitely overlooked. But as a person who likes having my brain tickled by quirky and creative ideas, web serials are the only form of fiction that has consistently pushed beyond the expected, and challenged my conceptions of how a story should behave. Brandon Sanderson is great, yes, but he doesn't come close.
