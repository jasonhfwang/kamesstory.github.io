My dad used to tell me he would toss books across the room just to force himself to stop reading, and that obsession definitely carried over to me. When I was young, I would lose days in novels. I probably spent more hours reading lore than actually playing any of the games they belonged to.
But my biggest storytelling addiction of all was web serials. Even now, after 15 years of quietly reading them, it's still a niche, and mentioning them tends to get polite smiles and general confusion. Frankly, they're overlooked. But I'm the type of person who chases ideas that tickle my mind, and web serials are the only form of fiction that has consistently pushed beyond the expected, and challenged my conceptions of how a story should behave. Brandon Sanderson? Not even close.
