feuil·le·ton
(noun) a part of a European newspaper devoted to light literature, fiction, criticism, etc.
I love serial fiction. Before they became literary classics, works like The Count of Monte Cristo, The Three Musketeers, and Great Expectations were published in regular installments, distributed in special sections of their patron newspapers. Readers were hooked —  — on the latest fiction drops.
Today's reflection of that history comes in the form of web serials. Authors who write on Patreon can make 10x the amount they would publishing novels traditionally, using platforms like Patreon (even discounting for their exorbitant creator fees). helping their authors reach incomes of what would now be $200,000+.
