Web serials are pretty underrepresented in the literary world, and I find that they're often dismissed of as shallower, less sophisticated imitations of published works. As a longtime participant in the scene, I've always found this to be so bizarre. Historically, widely-acclaimed literary works like The Count of Monte Cristo, The Three Musketeers, and Great Expectations were originally published as serials in newspaper feuilletons. Their modern-day counterparts live on the web, and creator platforms like Patreon enable an entire new generation of writers to have complete control over the style & direction of their stories, avoiding the high dependency of traditional writers on the approval and editorial interests of publishing companies. This has resulted in a variety of incredibly creative works, spanning genres and ideas that are not well-explored in the literary cosmos of published books.
